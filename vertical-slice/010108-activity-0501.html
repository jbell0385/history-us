Taking Perspectives - Aristotle and Rousseau

Around 350 B.C., Aristotle wrote his book Politics to try to answer the question about the role that politics should play in helping people live well. Through exploring Aristotle’s work in regard to these topics helps us to better understand the relationship between the political community, politics, and human affairs.

Enlightenment philosopher Jean Jacques Rousseau’s book The Social Contract, written in 1762, described the big idea of the will of the people. Rousseau’s work in this area has inspired many different instances of revolution as well as having helped democracies establish governments which are representative of the people being governed.
